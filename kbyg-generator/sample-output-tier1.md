### How to Qualify Leads — MQL vs SQL

Not every conversation is the same. Here's how to tell the difference and capture what matters:

**MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead)** — Interested and fits our target profile, but no specific buying intent yet.
- Visited the booth out of curiosity
- Attended our session
- Asked general questions about our product
- Examples: "I've heard about you guys" · "What do you do?" · "Interesting, tell me more"

**SQL (Sales Qualified Lead)** — Has a specific need, timeline, budget, or decision-making authority.
- Asked about pricing or implementation
- Described a problem our product solves
- Requested a follow-up meeting or demo
- Mentioned an active evaluation or competitive comparison
- Examples: "We're evaluating tools in this space" · "Can you do X?" · "I'd like to set up a call with my team"

**Questions to help you classify:**
- "What brought you to our booth?" → gauges intent
- "Are you currently evaluating solutions?" → gauges timeline
- "What's your role in this decision?" → gauges authority

**Scoring:** Tag every lead as **Hot** (wants meeting / active evaluation) · **Warm** (interested, good fit, no immediate timeline) · **Cool** (general interest, informational)

**Write notes that help the follow-up person.** Don't write "seemed interested." Write what they're interested IN, what problem they described, what they're currently using, and what they asked about. The salesperson should be able to pick up the conversation where you left off.

You're our intelligence team on the ground. Here's what to look for and how to share it.

- Capture: our booth (busy and quiet), our speakers on stage, team moments, creative competitor booths, interesting swag, great signage or activations
- Share to: #cloudvault-summit-2026-photos Slack channel

**At Other Booths — What to Notice**
- What messaging themes are competitors leading with?

- Booth staffing: how many people, how are they engaging visitors?
- Creative ideas: giveaways, interactive elements, signage, layout

**In Sessions — What to Listen For**
- Competitor product announcements or roadmap hints
