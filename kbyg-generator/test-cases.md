## Test Case 1: Tier 1 — Platinum Sponsor at Major Partner Conference

### Input

- **Event name:** CloudVault Summit 2026
- **Event dates:** October 14–17, 2026 (move-in Oct 13, move-out Oct 18)
- **Venue:** Las Vegas Convention Center, Las Vegas, NV
- **Event type:** Sponsored booth + speaking engagements + hosted events
- **Event tier:** Tier 1
- **Conference size:** 40,000 attendees
- **Attendee type:** Technical (primary), executive (secondary)
- **Focus area:** AI, cloud infrastructure, developer tools
- **Goals:** Generate 200+ qualified leads, deliver 50+ demos, secure 30 executive meetings, strengthen partnership visibility
- **Participation level:** Platinum sponsor — includes 20x30 booth, 3 meeting rooms, 2 main stage speaking slots, 1 sponsored event slot, attendee list access
- **Booth details:** Booth #1247, Hall B East Wing; 3 demo stations, meeting table, branded lounge
- **Booth images:**
  - Floor map showing booth location in Hall B (image provided)
  - Booth rendering from design team showing layout and asset placement (image provided)
- **Design assets:**
  - 2 retractable banners (8ft, new design for 2026)
  - Branded backdrop behind meeting table (reused from Q2 events, updated with new tagline)
  - 3 monitors running demo loop (Product A, Product B, Integration Demo)
  - 1 large LED screen showing customer testimonial reel
  - Printed materials: 500 product one-pagers, 200 technical spec sheets, 300 case study booklets
  - Swag: 1,000 branded notebooks, 500 premium t-shirts (for qualified leads only), 200 portable chargers (for meeting attendees)
  - Branded tablecloth, podium wrap, lanyards for staff
- **Hotel:** Bellagio, $229/night, group code CLOUDSUMMIT26, book by Sept 1
- **Hosted events:**
  - Executive Dinner: Tue 10/15 7–10pm, Bellagio Prime, capacity 25, led by [VP Sales]
  - Customer Happy Hour: Wed 10/16 5:30–7:30pm, booth lounge, open, led by [Marketing Dir]
  - Partner Roundtable: Thu 10/17 8–9:30am, Room B-201, capacity 15, led by [Partnerships Lead]
- **Sponsored events:**
  - Women in Cloud Breakfast: Wed 10/16 7:30–9am, Grand Ballroom C (logo, 2 seats, attendee list)
  - Developer Meetup: Wed 10/16 6–8pm, Innovation Hub (co-branded, 5-min intro slot)
- **Program sessions:**
  - "Building Responsible AI at Scale" — Tue 10/15 2–2:45pm, Main Stage B, [CTO]
  - "The Future of Enterprise AI" panel — Wed 10/16 11am–12pm, Room 304, [VP Product]
- **Highlighted events:**
  - Partner: CloudVault Keynote (Tue 9am), Partner Customer Awards Dinner (Wed 7pm)
  - Industry: "Enterprise AI Adoption" (Tue 3:15pm), "Security & Compliance in AI" (Wed 1pm), "Build vs Buy for AI Tools" (Thu 10am)
  - C-Suite: CTO Roundtable (Wed 8am, invite-only), CISO Reception (Wed 5pm)
- **Product updates:** v3.0 launched Sept 2026, new integration GA, beta feature at booth only
- **Lead capture:** Captello, app link provided, hot/warm/cool scoring
- **Staff:** 15 attending (roster with roles, expertise, shifts provided)
- **Post-event:** Debrief Wed Oct 22 2–3pm; leads due Mon Oct 20; eyes & ears due Fri Oct 18

### Expected Output Characteristics

- All 10 sections present at full depth, numbered 1–10 starting with Event Overview
- Expect a long, detailed document — all ten sections at full depth. Completeness matters more than page count.
- Floor map and booth rendering embedded in Section 2 with captions
- Design assets listed with placement and quantities
- Highlighted events section with partner, industry, and C-suite sub-groups
- Full MQL vs SQL training with qualifying questions
- Staff roster table with expertise and shift assignments
- "Who to pull in" routing guide
- All placeholders specific (no generic "TBD")
- Event Overview assembles data from source — no generated prose

## Test Case 2: Tier 2 — Speaking Engagement + Small Booth at Mid-Size Conference

### Input

- **Event name:** HealthTech Connect 2026
- **Event dates:** June 8–10, 2026
- **Venue:** McCormick Place, Chicago, IL
- **Event type:** Speaking engagement + sponsored booth
- **Event tier:** Tier 2
- **Conference size:** 2,000 attendees
- **Attendee type:** Mixed (healthcare IT leaders, clinical informaticists, vendor partners)
- **Focus area:** Healthcare IT, interoperability, AI in clinical settings
- **Goals:** Build brand awareness in healthcare vertical, generate 40+ leads, establish 3 partnership conversations
- **Participation level:** Silver sponsor — 10x10 booth, 1 speaking slot in breakout track
- **Booth details:** Booth #312, Main Hall; 1 demo station, small meeting table
- **Design assets:**
  - 1 retractable banner (reused, healthcare-specific messaging)
  - 1 monitor running product demo loop
  - Printed materials: 200 healthcare-specific one-pagers (new), 100 general case studies (reused)
  - Swag: 300 branded pens, 100 tote bags
- **Hotel:** Hyatt Regency McCormick, $189/night, book by May 15
- **No hosted events**
- **No sponsored events**
- **Program session:** "AI-Assisted Clinical Decision Support: Lessons from the Field" — Tue 6/9 2:30–3:15pm, Breakout Room 4B, [Solutions Engineer]
- **Highlighted events:**
  - Industry: Opening keynote (Mon 9am), "Interoperability Standards Update" (Tue 10am), "AI Regulation in Healthcare" panel (Wed 11am)
- **Product updates:** New healthcare module, 2 talking points
- **Lead capture:** Badge scanner provided by conference, basic lead form
- **Staff:** 5 attending (names, roles, contact provided; no formal shifts — all cover booth in rotation)
- **Post-event:** Notes due by June 16, informal debrief call June 18
- **Missing data:** No shipping info available yet, no competitor intel, no highlighted partner or C-suite events

### Expected Output Characteristics

- All 10 sections present but at Tier 2 depth
- Noticeably leaner than Tier 1 — same sections, less depth per section
- Design assets section brief — just key materials listed
- No hosted or sponsored events in calendar — section still present with program session and highlighted industry events only
- Brief MQL vs SQL definitions with a few questions
- Staff roster as simple table (name, role, contact) without shifts or routing guide
- Missing data handled with specific placeholders: "[PLANNER: Add shipping details when available]"
- No competitor analysis in eyes & ears — just photo guidance and general observation prompts
- Tone: same as Tier 1 but shorter throughout

## Test Case 3: Tier 3 — Small Regional Meetup, No Booth

### Input

- **Event name:** Midwest AI Leaders Roundtable
- **Event dates:** March 12, 2026 (single day)
- **Venue:** The Westin Cleveland, Cleveland, OH
- **Event type:** Attending only
- **Event tier:** Tier 3
- **Conference size:** 200 attendees
- **Attendee type:** Executive (CIOs, CTOs, VPs of Engineering from regional companies)
- **Focus area:** AI adoption strategy for mid-market enterprises
- **Goals:** Network with regional enterprise leaders, identify 5+ potential prospect conversations
- **Participation level:** Attending only — no booth, no sponsorship, no speaking
- **Booth details:** None
- **Design assets:** Business cards, 50 branded one-pagers (reused from previous event)
- **Hotel:** Not needed — driving distance for both attendees
- **No hosted, sponsored, or program events**
- **Product updates:** 1 paragraph summary of latest release
- **Lead capture:** Business cards + photos, log into shared spreadsheet after event
- **Staff:** 2 attending ([Name], Regional Sales Manager; [Name], Solutions Engineer)
- **Post-event:** Drop notes in #midwest-events Slack channel by March 14
- **Missing data:** No highlighted events (agenda not published yet), no travel details needed

### Expected Output Characteristics

- All 10 sections present but at minimal Tier 3 depth
- Short — 1–2 pages — because the event is small, not because detail was cut
- No booth details — "Our Presence" section notes we don't have a booth and lists materials we're bringing
- No hosted/sponsored/program calendar — section notes key sessions TBD with placeholder: "[PLANNER: Add key sessions once agenda is published]"
- No MQL/SQL training — just a reminder to capture name, company, interest, and follow-up priority
- Staff listed as two names with roles and work contact
- Post-event is a one-line reminder
- Contacts section is just planner + emergency
- Tone: brief, no wasted words, still professional

## Validation Checklist

After running each test case, verify:

- [ ] Sections numbered 1–10, starting with Event Overview
- [ ] Correct tier depth applied across all sections
- [ ] No section is empty — either populated, or has a specific placeholder
- [ ] Images embedded where provided (booth layout, floor maps, design asset photos) with captions
- [ ] Design assets included at appropriate depth for the tier
- [ ] Highlighted events section works correctly (present in T1/T2, minimal in T3)
- [ ] Lead qualification training scales correctly (full in T1, brief in T2, one-liner in T3)
- [ ] Staff section scales correctly (full roster in T1, simple table in T2, name list in T3)
- [ ] For Tier 3: Staff Attending and Key Contacts combined if team is very small
- [ ] Document length scales by tier (T1 most detailed → T3 minimal), driven by event scope, not a fixed page target
- [ ] No generated prose — all content assembled from source data
- [ ] All links and deadlines are bolded
- [ ] Document has title, event dates, version date, and "Questions?" line
